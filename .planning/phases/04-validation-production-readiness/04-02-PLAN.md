<objective>
**As a** developer, **I want to** cross-check simulation predictions against real match results, **so that** I can measure prediction accuracy with Brier score, Log Loss, and calibration.

This plan delivers three architectural moves in one vertical slice: (1) extraction of accuracy metric functions from WC to `football_core/evaluation.py` (Rule of Two), (2) validation CLI integration with `--validate` and `--api-key` flags on `ucl-predict`, and (3) accuracy display in the `–output` JSON. The validation cross-check uses `expected_score()` from Elo ratings (same foundation as the simulation engine) to derive per-match home-win probabilities for completed matches, then computes Brier, Log Loss, accuracy, and calibration ECE.

Purpose: Prove the engine generates reliable predictions by comparing them against real outcomes.
